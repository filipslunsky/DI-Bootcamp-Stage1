Imi's Life

  • on the 26th May 1910 Imi was born in Budapest, the Austro-hungarian Empire
  • in 1928 he won the Slovak Youth Wrestling Championship
  • in 1929 the adult championship in the light and middleweight divisions
  • in the late 1930s anti-Semitic riots threatened the Jewish population of Bratislava in Europe so Imi together with other Jewish boxers and wrestlers, helped to defend his Jewish neighborhood against fascist gangs
  • in 1935 Imi visited Mandatory Palestine with a team of Jewish wrestlers to participate in the Maccabiah Games but could not participate because of a broken rib that resulted from his training while en route. This led to the fundamental Krav Maga rule - “do not get hurt while training”
  • in 1940 Imi fled the rise of Nazism in Slovakia, heading for Palestine on the Aliyah Bet vessel, Pencho, which shipwrecked on the Dodecanese Islands in the Aegean Sea
  • in 1942 after serving with distinction in the British supervised Czechoslovak 11th Infantry Battalion in North Africa reached Palestine
  • in 1944 Lichtenfeld began training Haganah fighters in his areas of expertise: physical fitness, swimming, wrestling, use of the knife, and defenses against knife attacks
  • in 1948 when the State of Israel was founded and the IDF was formed, Lichtenfeld became Chief Instructor for Physical Fitness and Krav Maga at the IDF School of Combat Fitness
  • in 1964 Imi retired from the Israeli Military Forces
  • spent later years modifying Krav Maga to fit the needs of police forces and ordinary civilians mostly in Netanya and Tel Aviv, Israel
  • on 22 October 1978 he created the Israeli Krav Maga Association (IKMA)
  • in 1995 he founded the International Krav Maga Federation (IKMF)
  • on 9 January 1998 Lichtenfeld died in Netanya, Israel, at the age of 87
